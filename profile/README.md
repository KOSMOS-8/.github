# VC‑Match

### KOSMOS8 — The AI Pre‑Matching Platform for Venture

*Founders and investors screen, prepare, and qualify each other through AI — so a meeting only happens when it's truly worth it.*

</div>

---

## 🎯 The Problem

Both sides of a venture conversation waste enormous time before they can tell whether a meeting is worth having.

| For Founders | For Investors |
| --- | --- |
| Met many investors, few led anywhere. | ~95% of inbound BPs are dropped after the first call. |
| Re-pitching the same progress to everyone, over and over. | No way to know if a founder is worth meeting — but skipping means flying blind. |
| No idea what a given VC actually cares about before the meeting. | Too many hours sunk into low-quality first conversations. |

**The root issue is one thing seen from two sides: neither party can judge, *before* meeting, whether the meeting is worth it.**

---

## 🧭 The North Star

> **Founder initiates an NDA → Investor signs → the original BP is unlocked.**

When this happens, the platform has built enough trust between the two sides: the founder is willing to protect their core materials, the investor is willing to take on confidentiality, and both believe the relationship is worth advancing. Whether funding ultimately closes is *beyond* this milestone — **trust is the product.**

---

## ⚙️ How It Works

1. **Material Upload** — Founders upload BP, résumé and supporting docs; investors upload investment thesis and portfolio. The data foundation everything else runs on.
2. **Agent‑Built Profiles** — An agent reads the uploads and asks two layers of follow-ups: generic gaps first, then sector-specific deep dives. *Profile quality drives match quality — the question library is the moat.*
3. **BP Access Control** — The agent publishes a redacted summary while the original stays locked. Founders set the information boundary: what the agent may answer, and what must wait for a real conversation.
4. **Two‑Layer Matching Engine** — Layer 1 rule-based filtering (sector, stage, geography, check size); Layer 2 semantic matching (BP × thesis embeddings) → Top‑N candidate pairs.
5. **Auto‑Built VC Agents** — The system assembles an initial VC persona from public sources (website thesis, portfolio, interviews) — *no active effort required from scarce investor time.*
6. **Pre‑Conversation** — Agents stress-test the business logic and surface what each side truly cares about. The system then generates summaries: **Red Flags + a meeting-prep checklist** for the founder, **follow-up hotspots** for the investor.
7. **Mutual Like** — Only when *both* sides accept does the relationship advance.
8. **Shared Workspace** — A 1:1 room where the NDA is signed, the BP is unlocked, and meetings are booked via Google Calendar.
9. **Permit Authorization** — After signing, an investor can request a Permit; once granted, their agent auto-tracks the founder's progress summaries — so founders never re-pitch the same update. Revocable anytime.

## 👥 Who It's For

- **Founders** — early-stage builders raising capital or expanding their venture network.
- **Investors** — VCs and angels hunting quality deal flow and founders worth knowing.
- **Founder ↔ Founder** — peer matching by sector affinity and personality fit, for pure networking (no NDA, no BP).
